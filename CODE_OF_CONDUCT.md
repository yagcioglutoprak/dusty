# Code of Conduct

Dusty is a small open-source project about safe macOS cleanup. The project is
friendlier, safer, and more useful when people can ask questions or contribute
without getting dragged into hostile behavior.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, especially from first-time contributors.
- Keep criticism focused on the code, docs, safety model, or user experience.
- Explain safety concerns clearly and with enough detail for someone else to
  reproduce or understand them.
- Respect that cleanup tools touch sensitive local paths; do not ask people to
  share private file names, logs, or screenshots they are not comfortable making
  public.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized, discriminatory, or exclusionary language.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Pressuring users to reveal personal paths, local logs, or security details in
  public issues.
- Spam, star exchange, fake engagement, or low-effort promotion.

## Reporting

For ordinary project behavior concerns, open a GitHub issue or contact the
maintainer through the repository profile.

For private safety or deletion-model concerns, use GitHub's private
vulnerability reporting flow instead of a public issue. See
[.github/SECURITY.md](.github/SECURITY.md).

Reports will be reviewed by the maintainer. Responses may include clarifying
the issue, editing or removing content, closing or locking a thread, or blocking
accounts that repeatedly make the project unsafe for others.
